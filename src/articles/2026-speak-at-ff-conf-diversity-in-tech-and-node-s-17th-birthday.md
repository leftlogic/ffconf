## From Our Archive

Hellen also applied to our CFP last year and was selected to speak about ['Bias in our products: The case for diversity in tech'](https://ffconf.org/talks/2025_hellen/).

Her talk speaks to the unconventional routes into engineering that remain common for women and minorities, and how a legacy of historical bias and exclusionary job descriptions has produced a lack of diversity that surfaces in biased AI systems like facial recognition and discriminatory job-ad algorithms — making diverse teams essential at all stages.

If you can find yourself 30 minutes to watch it, it's well worth your time.

## Community News

Seventeen years ago yesterday, Node.js was released (though the timeline is even [longer](https://github.com/nodejs/node-v0.x-archive/commit/19478ed4b14263c489e872156ca55ff16a07ebe0). It was a privilege for Remy to be present at the first (and much-loved) [JSConf.EU](https://www.jsconf.eu/2009/speakers.html) when Ryan Dahl showed the [project in his talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ztspvPYybIY). This was a key project for the web, that also allowed many front end devs to pass their skills to the backend too.

The UK's first talk on the subject was when [Simon Willison spoke](https://simonwillison.net/2009/Nov/23/node/) at our inaugural FFConf in 2009 (when in fact he was supposed to be talking about JavaScript mashups - it's [still in the talk description](https://ffconf.org/talks/node/)!).

As a hat-tip to Node, here's just a few weird and wonderful projects we found:

- Real time monitoring system for an _actual_ [beehive](https://github.com/cepdnaclk/e19-3yp-beehive-monitoring-system) 🐝
- The story of how node [_nearly_ went to space](https://www.reaktor.com/insights-and-events/node-js-satellite-means-anyone-can-space-programmer) (though I know NASA's used Node before too) 🚀
- [Brewfactory](https://blog.risingstack.com/brewfactory-full-stack-homebrew-with-iot/) - the project that makes beer from code (sort of) 🍻

There's always going to be more, but we'd love to hear from you too. Reply to our newsletter or ping us on [Bluesky](https://bsky.app/profile/ffconf.org).
