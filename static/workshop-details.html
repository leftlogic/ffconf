Details for ffconf workshops 2018

Thank you for registering for my workshop this year. Please note that your workshop ticket also comes with a day conference pass for Thursday's ffconf and a separate email has been sent that contains the day conference details.

Below are the details for both workshops running on the Wednesday 7th November 2018.

IMPORTANT Cliff notes (for those email skimmers - like me!):

  • Bring a charged laptop and your own power adapters
  • 9:30 registration at Lighthouse, 28 Kensington St, Brighton BN1 4AJ
  • Lunch, coffee, etc is provided (any dietary requirements - let us know ASAP)
  • The require you have the latest (stable) version of Node and npm (installation here)
  • Below is everything you need to know for the the workshop. If you have any questions, please email me and we'll get back as quickly as possible. Otherwise, I'll see you next week!

Directions

How to get there: https://ffconf.org/workshop

The workshop is being held at Lighthouse which is located less than 10 minutes walk from Brighton train station.

Trains run regularly from Gatwick Airport, London Bridge and London Victoria stations. We recommend using http://www.traintimes.org.uk for up to date timetables. Hotel recommendations can be found on the ffconf website.

Registration

Registration opens at 9:30am. There are no physical tickets for the workshop or conference, but you must bring a form of identification (bank card, driving licence etc) to pick up your delegate programme which also acts as your pass. Please keep your pass with you at all times as you will need it to get back into the workshop.

You will also need this pass for the conference on Thursday, so please keep it safe!

What you'll need to bring

You will need to bring your own laptop and power supply (and power adapters if you're travelling from abroad). The workshops will include practical work and to get the most from the day you will need to bring a computer.

The workshop require that you have the latest stable version of node installed (version 10) and npm (which comes with the node installation). To download and install fresh use this link: https://nodejs.org/en/download/

It's also work running the following commands to save having to download over the workshop wifi:

mkdir next-workshop
cd next-workshop
npm init -f
npm i react react-dom next

Schedule

The workshop is broken into four parts:

  • 09:30 - registration (coffee, tea & biscuits provided)
  • 10:00 - workshop begins
  • 11:00 - 30 minute break and refreshments (tea & coffee)
  • 13:00 - 60 minute lunch break, lunch is provided if you have dietary requirements please let us know ASAP
  • 15:00 - 30 minute break and refreshments (tea & coffee)
  • 17:00 - workshop finishes

Post workshop / side event

Asyncjs: After the workshop, there will be a special International Show'n'Tell edition of Async, our local JavaScript meetup in Brighton.

https://asyncjs.com/

Everybody is welcome, just sign up. Furthermore, if you have some little project you've been working on or some new technology you're excited about, you can grab yourself a 5 minute speaker's slot.

The event will be held at The Skiff (map at the bottom of the link); doors open at 7.15pm.

Again, If you have any questions before the workshops, please get in touch and we'll do our best to help.

Looking forward to seeing you all next week.
